The problem

Your students know AI. But can they build it?

Most students today have used a cloud-based AI tool. Very few have ever trained a model, labelled a dataset, or run inference on real hardware. That gap - between using and building - is the gap between being a consumer of the AI economy and a creator inside it.

What most schools teach as "AI"
  • Slides explaining what AI is
  • Demos of cloud-based AI tools
  • Drag-and-drop block coding
  • Theory without hardware
  • No exposure to datasets or model training
What AI careers actually require
  • Hands-on training of real models
  • Working with datasets - collection, labelling, cleaning
  • Inference on edge devices and GPUs
  • Python, PyTorch, model evaluation
  • Building a project the student owns end-to-end

How we teach

From first exposure to a working model - in four stages.

Every stage is hands-on. Every stage is on real hardware. No simulators, no toy demos.

01

See It

Students watch a live AI model run on real hardware - perception, classification, decision in real time.

02

Understand It

We open the model. What is a neural network doing? What data trained it? Where does it go wrong?

03

Train It

Students collect data, label it, and train a model end-to-end on GPU - the same workflow industry uses.

04

Build With It

Students ship a working AI project - deployed to an edge device, solving a problem they chose. Theirs to keep.

What students build

Real projects. Indian context. Portfolio-grade.

A sample of the projects student cohorts have built or are scoped to build - chosen for their relevance to Indian classrooms, communities, and careers.

Vision · Agriculture

Crop Disease Detector

Edge model that identifies leaf disease in wheat and tomato from a phone camera - trained on a student-collected dataset.

Speech · Indian Languages

Hindi Voice Command Recognizer

A small speech model that recognizes 20 Hindi voice commands offline - runs on a sub-1W edge board.

Vision · Public Safety

Traffic Sign Classifier

An on-device model that recognizes Indian road signs from dashcam footage - first step toward driver-assist research.

Sensors · Environment

Air Quality Predictor

A regression model trained on local AQI sensor data, predicting next-hour pollution for a student's neighbourhood.

Number Plate OCR

A two-stage model: detect plate, read characters - all running offline on a credit-card-sized edge device.
